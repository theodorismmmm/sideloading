The Basics

What is Sideloading?

Sideloading lets you install apps on your iPhone or iPad outside the official App Store — giving you access to tweaked apps, emulators, custom tools, and more.

📖 How it Works

Apple normally only allows apps installed through the App Store. Sideloading bypasses this restriction by signing an app with a valid Apple Developer certificate that has your device's unique identifier (UDID) embedded into it. Because the certificate is issued by Apple, iOS trusts and runs the app just like any other.

The key components are a P12 certificate (your signing identity) and a signer tool (the software that applies the signature to an IPA file). Together, they turn any IPA file into an app your iPhone is happy to run.

🔍 Common Use Cases

  • → Tweaked or modified versions of popular apps
  • → Retro game emulators not available on the App Store
  • → Productivity tools with features Apple has restricted
  • → Beta or self-developed applications
  • → AltStore, Sideloadly, or custom signing solutions

✅ Pros of Sideloading

  • Access apps not on the App Store
  • Install tweaked or modified apps
  • No jailbreak required
  • Apps run natively on iOS
  • Great for developers & power users
  • Certificate tied to your device only

❌ Cons of Sideloading

  • Certificate can be revoked by Apple
  • Certificate is valid for 1 year only
  • Violates Apple's Terms of Service
  • Apps may stop working after iOS updates
  • Risk of installing untrusted software
  • Requires re-signing after revocation
The Tools

P12 Certificate & Signer Explained

Two pieces of technology work together to make sideloading possible. Here's what they are and why you need both.

🔑

What is a P12 Certificate?

A P12 (PKCS #12) file is a bundle that contains your Apple Developer signing identity — essentially a cryptographic key pair issued by Apple. It proves to iOS that an app has been legitimately signed by a registered developer.

Think of it as a wax seal on a letter: iOS opens the app only if it sees a trusted seal. Without a valid P12, your iPhone will refuse to launch the app.

Why do you need it?

  • Required by iOS to verify app authenticity
  • Links the app to a specific Apple developer account
  • Embeds your device's UDID so only your device can run it
  • Apple-issued, making iOS treat it as trusted
  • Valid for 12 months before renewal is required
✍️

What is a Signer?

A signer is a piece of software (or a service) that takes an IPA file — the raw iPhone app package — and re-signs it using your P12 certificate and provisioning profile. Without a signer, the P12 certificate by itself does nothing.

Popular signers include AltStore, Sideloadly, Esign, and GBox. We help you set up and configure the right one for your workflow.

What can you do with a signer?

  • Install any IPA file on your device
  • Re-sign apps after a revocation or update
  • Manage multiple signed apps from one place
  • Refresh app signatures before they expire
  • Sideload apps over Wi-Fi without a computer (some tools)
  • Run tweaked, patched, or developer-only apps

Getting Your UDID (Device ID)

We ask you to provide your device's UDID (Unique Device Identifier). This is a 25-character alphanumeric code that uniquely identifies your iPhone or iPad within Apple's systems.

Why is the UDID required?
Apple's provisioning system ties a certificate to specific registered device UDIDs. By registering your UDID — and only yours — we ensure:

  • → The certificate is locked to your device only. No one else can use it.
  • → Your signing identity cannot be stolen and used on other devices.
  • → Apple will only validate signed apps on that one registered device within their infrastructure.
Your UDID stays private — we never share it

⚠ Important Warnings

  • Certificates are valid for 1 year only. After 12 months the certificate expires and you will need to renew.
  • Apple can revoke a certificate at any time, without notice. This is outside our control and is an inherent risk of sideloading.
  • Revocation causes all apps signed with that certificate to immediately stop working on your device.
  • Using sideloaded apps may violate Apple's Terms of Service. Proceed at your own discretion.
  • We strongly advise against using sideloaded apps for sensitive operations (banking, etc.).
